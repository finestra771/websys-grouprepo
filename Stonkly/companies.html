Terminology

*Initial Public Offering (IPO): The process in which a company decides to "go public" by selling shares of stock to the public. In essence the company's ownership is transitioning from a private ownership to a public one.

**Listed Exchange: Trading venue where an asset is officially made availible for public trading.

***Market Capitalization (Market Cap): The current market value of all the company's outstanding stock shares. It's used to indicate a company's size and worth in comparison to its peers.

****Outstanding Shares: The amount of company shares that are traded on the secondary market, ie the number of shares that are availible to investors. These include all restricted shares held by the company's officers and insiders, as well as the quity portion owned by institutional investors.
